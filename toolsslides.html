Mercurial Barometer

In a mercurial barometer, it uses a column of mercury balanced by atmospheric pressure, with corrections for temperature, gravity, and latitude. Measures pressure in millibars, millimeters, or inches of mercury.

Aneroid Barometer

An aneroid barometer consists of a sealed box, often referred to as an aneroid cell and typically blue, enclosing the mechanism. This mechanism expands or contracts with changing pressure, thereby adjusting a pointer on the yellow dial through the action of a spring and a series of levers.

Know More

Barograph

A barograph is a recording barometer and it records atmospheric pressure over time using a pen point moved by a series of levers connected to aneroid cells, providing a more pronounced response to pressure changes.
